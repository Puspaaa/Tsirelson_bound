Welcome back. Speaker 1 set up the landscape of correlations. Now I pick up from where they left off — we've seen the hierarchy, we've seen the CHSH game. Now let's ask: what exactly IS Tsirelson's bound, what violates it, and what fundamental law prevents Nature from going further?

Tsirelson's Bound (1980)

Theorem. For any quantum strategy using shared entanglement:

$2 - \sqrt{2} \;\leq\; S \;\leq\; 2 + \sqrt{2}$

where the CHSH score is defined as:

$S = \displaystyle\sum_{x,y \in \{0,1\}} P(a \oplus b = x \cdot y \mid x, y)$

Classical limit: $S = 2$
Quantum limit: $S = 2\sqrt{2} \approx 2.83$
No-signaling: $S = 4$ (algebraic max)

Why does nature stop at $2\sqrt{2}$ and not at $4$?

Tsirelson's bound tells us that quantum mechanics can outperform classical physics in the CHSH game — but only up to a point. The maximum quantum CHSH score is 2 plus root 2, approximately 2.83. This is strictly less than the algebraic maximum of 4 allowed by no-signaling. The PR-Box Popescu & Rohrlich, 1994

A hypothetical device with the perfect correlation rule:

$a \oplus b = x \cdot y$

Always satisfied — deterministically, for every input pair

Winning probability: $P(\text{win}) = 1$ for all inputs $(x,y)$

CHSH score: $S = 4$ — the algebraic maximum

No-signaling? Yes! Marginals are uniform: $P(a|x) = P(b|y) = \tfrac{1}{2}$

If it doesn't violate relativity, why doesn't it exist?

The PR-box is a thought experiment: a hypothetical device that satisfies a-XOR-b equals x-AND-y for EVERY input pair, achieving a perfect CHSH score of 4. It respects no-signaling — the marginals are completely uniform, so neither Alice nor Bob can signal. Yet it obliterates Tsirelson's bound. The animation builds a truth table row by row, showing all four input combinations winning perfectly, then reveals the S=4 score with a VIOLATION stamp. If no-signaling isn't enough to exclude it, what law of physics does?

Information Causality Pawłowski et al., 2009

A physical principle constraining correlations:

IC Principle. If Alice sends $m$ bits to Bob:

$I \;=\; \displaystyle\sum_{i=0}^{N-1} I_{\text{Sh}}(a_i : \beta \mid b = i) \;\leq\; m$

The game: Alice has $N$ random bits $(a_0, \ldots, a_{N-1})$. She sends $m$ bits to Bob. Bob receives random index $b$ and must guess $a_b$.

Meaning: Total accessible information $\leq$ bits communicated. No free lunch from shared resources!

Quantum

$I \leq m$

✓ PASSES

PR-Box

$I > m$

✗ FAILS

Information Causality is the key insight from Pawlowski et al. The total information Bob can access about Alice's data cannot exceed the number of classical bits she sent — even with shared entanglement or any no-signaling resource. Quantum mechanics satisfies this beautifully. But the PR-box violates it: with a PR-box, Bob could extract more information than Alice sent, which would be a form of superluminal information transfer dressed up in correlations. General Entropy & DPI

Dahlsten, Lercher & Renner show that three natural axioms imply Tsirelson's bound:

COND — Conditioning on measurement outcomes gives valid sub-states

SHAN — Entropy of classical states equals Shannon entropy

DPI — Data-processing inequality: $H(A \mid B) \leq H(A \mid T(B))$

Where the generalized entropy is:

$H(\vec{P}) = \min_{e \in \mathcal{M}^*} H_{\text{Sh}}\!\bigl(e(\vec{P})\bigr)$

Minimized over all fine-grained measurements

DPI

+ COND + SHAN

⟹

IC

$I \leq m$

⟹

Tsirelson

$S \leq 2\sqrt{2}$

This is the core result of the paper. Dahlsten, Lercher and Renner define a generalized entropy — the minimum Shannon entropy over all fine-grained measurements — and show it satisfies the Data Processing Inequality in quantum mechanics. Combined with two other mild axioms (COND and SHAN), the DPI implies Information Causality, which in turn implies Tsirelson's bound. The animation visualizes how different measurements give different Shannon entropies, and the generalized entropy picks the minimum. The chain at the bottom shows the logical flow: DPI implies IC implies Tsirelson's bound.

The Logical Chain

How a simple information-processing principle excludes super-quantum correlations

DPI ⟹ Information Causality ⟹ Tsirelson's Bound ⟹ PR-Box excluded

The real constraint is information-theoretic, not just relativistic!

This slide shows the full animated logical chain. The DPI — a fundamental property of information processing — combined with two mild axioms, implies Information Causality. Information Causality then implies Tsirelson's bound, which excludes super-quantum correlations like the PR-box. The key takeaway: the limit of quantum correlations isn't just an accident of the formalism. It's a consequence of how information fundamentally works. No-signaling alone isn't enough — you need the DPI. This completes the story of "the crime and the law."
